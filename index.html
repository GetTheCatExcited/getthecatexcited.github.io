  • Generating sustainable growth.

    OEMs face limited growth, with most reporting below-average EBIT and lower-than-expected valuations. Shareholders find more attractive options in higher-valued sectors, making sustained profitability a challenge.

  • Enhancing product excellence through customer-centric solutions.

    OEMs struggle to integrate customer-centric solutions, resulting in lower performance compared to tech companies, which achieve higher profitability and valuation through business innovation and customer focus

  • Commercializing new powertrains.

    Truck OEMs risk overinvesting in electrification and hydrogen powertrains due to lagging demand. Ambitious 2030 sales targets exceed forecasts, while EU regulations widen the investment-demand gap, requiring strategic decision-making and customer focus.

  • Building resilience against cyber threats and other risks.

    Cyber insecurity poses a major global risk for international companies, amplified by generative AI. The transportation sector's digital reliance increases vulnerability, requiring OEMs to manage these risks alongside financial and geopolitical challenges.

  • Driving impact through new technology.

    New technology can enhance customer engagement, operational efficiency, and workforce empowerment, but traditional industries struggle to leverage these solutions for business growth.
